Equal Employment

In addition to barring discriminatory voter registration practices, the Civil Rights Act of 1964 also aimed to eliminate discrimination in the workplace. This section will explore how the Civil Rights Act created more opportunities for employment for African Americans.

Background

  • Before the Civil Rights Act of 1964, employers openly discriminated against African Americans
  • African Americans were denied equal employment opportunities based on their race
    Employers did not consider African Americans for positions solely based on the color of their skin
  • African Americans were relegated to low-paying jobs
  • They had little to no chance for advancement.
  • Kathleen O'Toole's essay in 2000 described the Civil Rights Act as a "market-enhancing intervention" (O’Toole)
  • The Act removed uncertainty and allowed free consumer commerce to flourish
  • The Act had a positive impact on the economy and created a more level playing field for African Americans in the economy
  • It also increase in job opportunities for African Americans, particularly in skilled professions.
(Cente)
  • A study from the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission shows that African American participation in skilled professions increased significantly after the Civil Rights Act
  • In 1966, African American professionals reported a participation rate of 1.32 percent and the participation rate increased to 7.75 percent by 2008 (United States Equal Employment Opportunity Commission)
  • The Act opened up more opportunities for African Americans to access and compete for jobs that were previously dominated by white workers.

Why was this a frontier?

By prohibiting employment discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, or national origin, the Civil Rights Act of 1964 was a frontier for African Americans in creating more opportunities for employment. It opened new doors for African Americans to access skilled jobs that were previously closed off to them, allowing them to attain higher paying, more stable jobs and increase their social and economic mobility.
